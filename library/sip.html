What is SIP?(Systematic Investment Plan)

A Systematic Investment Plan or SIP is an investment vehicle through which investors can invest specific amounts of money in mutual funds. This is done periodically such as monthly, quarterly or annually. By planting small seeds of investment month after month, the investor can hope to gain the fruits of investment returns over a period of time. Investing through SIP is a disciplined approach that helps investors to achieve your financial goals.

Why to invest SIP?

There are many benefits of SIP. This mode of investment in mutual fund entails

Ease of investment
You can easily set up a SIP, offline or online, and start investing in your chosen fund. Most mutual funds allow SIP with as little as ₹500 per month. Upon receiving the instruction, the said amount is deducted from your bank account and invested in the fund on the given date.
Brings discipline into investments
This is one of the major advantages of SIP. It brings discipline into your investments. SIPs are a kind of forced savings. On a pre-defined date, a certain amount is deducted and invested on your behalf. Over a period this inculcates discipline, crucial for long-term wealth creation.
Spreads out risk
Mutual fund investments are subject to market risks. However, SIPs spread this risk over a period, thus bringing down the overall risk of investment. Also, SIPs help you remain invested across market cycles, thus averaging out the cost of buying.
Gives the flexibility to top-up
This is one of the multiple benefits of SIP. With a rise in income, you can top-up your SIP amount. A SIP top-up can make a significant difference to the end corpus. Also, most fund houses offer the facility to pause your SIPs for a brief period in case you face shortage of funds.
How to invest SIP?

Now that you know why SIPs, let’s see how you can invest through SIPs. To do so, you need to:

Set investment goals
Note that every mutual fund serves a specific goal. Hence, you need to figure out the objective you want to achieve by investing in mutual funds through SIPs.
Pick the mutual fund that serves the goal
Once you have set your investment goal, choose the mutual fund that can help you achieve it. It’s advisable to opt for a fund with sound fundamentals.
Get KYC done and start your SIP
If you are investing in mutual funds for the first time, you need to be KYC-compliant, mandatory under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act. You can get the KYC done, either offline or online. For offline process, download the KYC form available on website of mutual fund houses, registrar and transfer agents (RTAs), or the Association of Mutual Funds in India (AMFI).

Fill and submit it to the fund’s house office or RTA office. You can do KYC online through your Aadhaar number. The facility for the same is provided on the portal of fund houses and RTAs.
Benefits of investing through SIPs

There are multiple benefits of investing in mutual funds through SIPs.

The power of compounding

When you invest in an SIP, you earn regular returns. If these returns are reinvested in the fund, the returns start earning returns. This is known as compounding. In other words, the money you earn in turn starts earning money for you. This can help you achieve much higher returns over a period of time.

Rupee cost averaging

Rupee Cost Averaging is another major advantage of investing in SIPs. As an investor, it can be tough to time the market; especially when the market is volatile. By investing through SIP, you don’t need to worry about timing the market.

Rupee cost averaging decreases your overall cost per share of an investment. This means you can avail more units of an investment when the price is low and higher returns when the price of the fund rises.

Convenience

SIP is a very convenient mode of investing. Most investors don’t have the time for extensive research and analysis to pick up the best stocks in the market. Once a fund is picked, the investor can simply give a standing instruction to the bank and the specific amount is debited each and every month for investment in the SIP. In other words, it is a hassle free option that helps investors to finance their dreams in a simple and convenient way.
