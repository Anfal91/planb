What is SWP?(Systematic Withdrawal Plan)

A Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) is a facility offered by Mutual Fund, which allows an investor to periodically withdraw fixed money as per requirement instead of withdrawing it in one go. Withdrawals through SWP are subject to exit load and tax implications.

HOW DOES THE SWP IN MUTUAL FUNDS WORK?
Step 1 - Your mutual fund investments have accumulated to a sizeable sum over long-term.
Step 2 - You need monthly/ quarterly cash flows for specific needs.
Step 3 - You register a SWP to withdraw a fixed amount/ number of units per month or quarter.
Step 4 - Mutual fund redeems the desired amount into your bank account periodically.
WHAT KIND OF AN INVESTOR SHOULD INVEST IN THE SWP?
Investor looking to fulfil monthly / quarterly cash flow requirements. This generally happens during the retirement phase or in case of liquidity crunch, wherein you intend to live off the accumulated corpus. Business persons / Self-employed professionals having irregular income flow can also use SWP for managing their monthly fixed expenses.
Benefits of SWP

SWP helps you with systematic redemption of your invested money based on your liquidity need. It allows you to have fixed income every month in your bank account. It allows you to plan your goals through systematic withdrawals on periodic basis. Systematic withdrawal plan is best suited for retired people which allows them to withdraw / or have fixed amount every month from their invested amount without withdrawing the entire investment amount.
