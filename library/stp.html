What is STP?(Systematic Transfer Plan)

Systematic Transfer Plan or STP is an investment plan that allows investors to invest a lump sum amount in a particular mutual fund and regularly transfer a specific amount into another fund. For instance, the investor can choose to transfer a pre-defined amount from a debt fund to an equity fund (or vice versa) on a regular basis. For this to happen, the units from one fund are sold and the money is used to purchase units in the equity fund.

How does it work?
Let’s take an example to understand how STPs actually work in the market. Gaurav is a mutual fund investor. He has currently invested Rs 2 lakh in a debt fund. He has his eye on a particular equity fund. However, due to the volatility in the market, he is afraid that the value of the equity fund may decrease after he invests in it. But if the equity fund’s value starts to rise, he could lose out on a potentially good investment.

Instead, by investing through an STP, he can gradually transfer a specific amount (Rs 8,000 in this example) from his debt fund to equity fund over the span of a few months (or years).

This gradual transfer of money can minimise his risk in the equity market while helping him earn quality returns at the same time.
Advantage of investing through STP

Safety
By investing only a portion of your funds at a time in equity, you can shield your investments from market swings. This way, the entire fund won’t be exposed to the volatility in the market.

High returns
The main purpose of investing in equity is to earn higher returns. The equity portion of the fund can help investors earn higher returns over the long-term. At the same time, you can earn consistent returns from debt or liquid fund.

Portfolio rebalance
Systematic Transfer Plans (STPs) help investors to rebalance their portfolios by transferring the investments from one fund to another. For instance, if the investor feels that the portfolio is heavily into equities, he can start an STP to switch funds back into debt (and vice versa).

Conclusion

STP is a very useful strategy to mitigate risks in the market. But it is necessary to remember that it doesn’t eliminate the possibility of losses in the market. At the same time, the minimisation of risk exposure means that the investor could lose out on potential returns when the market performs exceedingly well. However, this strategy can be used to cap losses and increase portfolio returns in the long-term.
